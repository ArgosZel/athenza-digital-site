Athenza Digital - Data Privacy Policy (GDPR Compliant)

Version: 1.0 | Effective Date: April 18, 2026 | Applicability: All Data Processing Activities

1. Introduction

1.1 Purpose

This Data Privacy Policy outlines how Athenza Digital collects, uses, processes, stores, and protects personal data in compliance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and other applicable data protection laws.

1.2 Scope

This policy applies to: - All personal data processed by Athenza Digital - All employees, contractors, and third‑party processors - All processing activities, regardless of location

1.3 Definitions

  • Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person
  • Processing: Any operation performed on personal data
  • Data Controller: Determines purposes and means of processing
  • Data Processor: Processes data on behalf of controller
  • Data Subject: Individual whose data is processed

1.4 Principles

We adhere to GDPR principles: - Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency - Purpose limitation - Data minimization - Accuracy - Storage limitation - Integrity and confidentiality - Accountability

2. Data Collection & Processing

2.1 Data We Collect

2.1.1 Website Visitors (athenzadigital.com)

Collected via contact form: - Name - Email address - Website URL - Optional message

Automatically collected: - IP address - Browser type and version - Device information - Pages visited, time spent - Referring website

2.1.2 Clients & Prospects

Business contact information: - Name, job title, company - Email, phone number, address - Communication history - Contract details

Project‑related data: - Website access credentials (temporary) - Analytics data - Performance metrics - Feedback and survey responses

2.1.3 Employees & Contractors

Employment data: - Contact information - Identification documents - Bank details (for payment) - Performance records - Training records

2.2 Lawful Basis for Processing

Processing Activity Lawful Basis Documentation

2.3 Purpose Limitation

We process data only for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes: - Service delivery and project execution - Communication with clients and prospects - Website analytics and improvement - Legal and regulatory compliance - Business administration

3. Data Subject Rights

3.1 Right to Information

Data subjects have the right to know: - What data we hold about them - Why we process it - Who we share it with - How long we keep it

Response time: Within 30 days of request

3.2 Right of Access

Data subjects may request access to their personal data.

Process: 1. Submit request via email to info@athenzadigital.com 2. Identity verification (to prevent unauthorized access) 3. Data provided in commonly used electronic format

Fee: None, unless requests are manifestly unfounded or excessive

3.3 Right to Rectification

Data subjects may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.

Process: - Request via email - Verification of identity - Correction within 30 days - Notification of corrections to third parties (if applicable)

3.4 Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten")

Data subjects may request deletion of their data when: - Data is no longer necessary for its purpose - Consent is withdrawn (and no other lawful basis exists) - Data was processed unlawfully - Legal obligation requires deletion

Exceptions: Legal requirements, exercise or defense of legal claims

3.5 Right to Restriction of Processing

Data subjects may request restriction when: - Accuracy is contested (during verification period) - Processing is unlawful but erasure not requested - We no longer need data but subject needs it for legal claims - Objection to processing is under consideration

3.6 Right to Data Portability

Data subjects may receive their data in structured, commonly used, machine‑readable format and transmit it to another controller.

Applies to: Data provided by subject, processed by automated means, based on consent or contract

3.7 Right to Object

Data subjects may object to processing based on legitimate interests.

Direct marketing: Absolute right to object, processed immediately

Other processing: We will demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override interests, rights, and freedoms

3.8 Rights Related to Automated Decision‑Making

We do not engage in automated decision‑making with legal or significant effects.

3.9 How to Exercise Rights

Contact: info@athenzadigital.com
Verification: Identity confirmation required
Response time: 30 days (may extend to 60 for complex requests)

4. Data Security

4.1 Technical Measures

Encryption: - Data in transit: TLS 1.2+ - Data at rest: AES‑256 encryption - Database encryption for sensitive fields

Access Control: - Principle of least privilege - Unique user accounts - Two‑factor authentication where available - Regular access reviews

Network Security: - Firewalls and intrusion detection - Regular security updates - VPN for remote access - Secure configuration standards

4.2 Organizational Measures

Policies & Training: - Regular security awareness training - Clear security policies - Incident response plan - Regular risk assessments

Physical Security: - Secure premises (for any physical locations) - Locked cabinets for physical documents - Secure disposal of media

4.3 Vendor Management

Third‑party processors must: - Provide GDPR compliance assurances - Sign Data Processing Agreements - Allow security audits (on request) - Notify us of breaches

Current processors: [List maintained separately]

5. Data Breach Response

5.1 Detection & Assessment

All employees must report suspected breaches immediately to: info@athenzadigital.com

Assessment within 24 hours: - Nature of breach - Categories of data affected - Number of data subjects affected - Likely consequences - Measures taken/proposed

5.2 Notification Requirements

To supervisory authority: Within 72 hours of awareness, unless low risk to rights/freedoms

To data subjects: Without undue delay when high risk to rights/freedoms

Notification content: - Nature of breach - Contact details of DPO/contact point - Likely consequences - Measures taken/proposed

5.3 Containment & Recovery

Immediate actions: - Isolate affected systems - Preserve evidence - Engage forensic experts (if needed) - Implement corrective measures

Long‑term actions: - Root cause analysis - Process improvements - Additional safeguards - Training updates
